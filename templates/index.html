Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert HEIC to JPG on Windows?
Simply visit heictojpg and drop your HEIC files into the converter. It works entirely in your browser — no software to install on Windows. Just drop your files, click Convert All, and download your JPGs instantly.
Why can't Windows open my iPhone photos?
iPhones save photos in HEIC format by default, which Windows doesn't natively support without a codec pack. Converting them to JPG makes them universally compatible with Windows, Chrome, and any app or website.
Is it safe to convert HEIC files online?
Yes — on heictojpg, your photos never leave your device. All conversion happens entirely in your browser using WebAssembly. No server ever sees your files, making it completely private.
How do I convert multiple HEIC files at once?
Drop up to 200 HEIC files at once into the drop zone, then click Convert All. When all files are done, use the Download All ZIP button to get every converted file packaged in one archive.
What is a HEIC file?
HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's modern image format introduced with iOS 11. It uses the HEVC codec to deliver better quality at roughly half the file size of a standard JPG. Most non-Apple software doesn't support it natively.
How do I turn off HEIC on my iPhone?
Go to Settings → Camera → Formats and select "Most Compatible." Your iPhone will then save photos as standard JPG files. This uses more storage but ensures photos open on any device without conversion.
Can I convert HEIC to JPG without internet?
Yes — once the heictojpg page has fully loaded, it works completely offline. The WebAssembly converter runs locally in your browser with no server connection required for conversion.
Does this work on Mac?
Yes, heictojpg works on Mac in Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge. Drag and drop your HEIC files directly into the browser window, choose your output format, and download the converted images — no software installation needed.
